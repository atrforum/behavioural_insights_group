About

Established in October 2025, the Committee for Behavioural Insights (CBI) is a standing group of the Australasian Transport Research Forum (ATRF). The group brings together researchers, practitioners and industry leaders from across Australia and New Zealand who share an interest in how behavioural insights can improve transport research, policy and practice.

The purpose of the ATRF Committee for Behavioural Insights is to promote and improve the quality of behavioural insights research in Australia and New Zealand, and to advocate for the incorporation of evidence based behavioural methods and insights into transport and land-use planning and analysis, design, and implementation.

The group's activities span methods, applications, outcomes, communication, advocacy and a "state of the nation" view of behavioural insights work across Australasia. We meet online monthly, run quarterly hybrid presentations with invited guest speakers, and hold an annual in-person workshop on the sidelines of the ATRF conference.

ATRF-CBI is co-chaired by Kate Mackay and Liz Ampt. Durba Kundu is the Secretary, Justine Smith and Yuting Zhang are the Research Coordinators, and Alex Iljin and Leonie Gibbons are the Events Coordinators. Membership is intentionally diverse — spanning government, consultancy, academia and industry — so that the group's work reflects the breadth of practice in the region.

To become a member or friend of ATRF-CBI, contact Kate Mackay or Liz Ampt, the co-chairs. All members of ATRF-CBI must be members of ATRF (it's free).

About

Established in October 2025, the Behavioural Insights Group (BIG) is a standing group of the Australasian Transport Research Forum (ATRF). The group brings together researchers, practitioners and industry leaders from across Australia and New Zealand who share an interest in how behavioural insights can improve transport research, policy and practice. Anyone can become a member of ATRF by applying here (it's free).

The aim of ATRF-BIG is to promote and improve the quality of behavioural insights research in Australia and New Zealand, build the evidence base for its application, and explore the incorporation of behavioural insights into transport and land-use approaches.

The group's activities span methods, applications, outcomes, communication, advocacy and a "state of the nation" view of behavioural insights work across Australasia. We meet online monthly, run quarterly hybrid presentations with invited guest speakers, and hold an annual in-person workshop on the sidelines of the ATRF conference.

ATRF-BIG is co-chaired by Kate Mackay and Liz Ampt. Justine Smith and Yuting Zhang are the Research Coordinators, and Alex Iljin and Leonie Gibbons are the Events Coordinators. Membership is intentionally diverse — spanning government, consultancy, academia and industry — so that the group's work reflects the breadth of practice in the region.

To become a member or friend of ATRF-BIG, contact a co-chair. All members of ATRF-BIG must be members of ATRF (it's free).
